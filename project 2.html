Developing a reliable and scalable crop detection model for small holder farming systems in Africa

Producing accurate crop maps in the sub-Saharan region is a challenging task as it mostly consists of small holder farms as compared to different areas of the World. The small size of the croplands makes it incredibly difficult to assess farm boundaries using lower resolution imagery. [] The images below display the ESA world cover map for western France (top) and western Kenya(bottom). A lot of fields get confused for cropland in Kenya but not in France.

Another difficulty with this region is the overlap between the cropping seasons. Due to the presence of a long and short season, it is difficult to delineate the multiple crop signatures from each other. In the figure below, we see that the 2 major seasons in Kenya are overlapping but are separate in India.
